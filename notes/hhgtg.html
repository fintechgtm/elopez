The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy Book Review: "Space Travel Meets Alice in Wonderland"

I just finished reading "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" and thought it was a fun and enjoyable read. I had trouble getting into the story at first because I didn't realize it was a satire on the sci-fi genre. But once I understood that, I found it much more enjoyable. If you’re looking for a deep read or traditional sci-fi, this isn’t it. But it is a great choice for a laugh or two.

The story is about a guy named Arthur Dent who gets swept up on a wild adventure through space with his friend Ford Prefect. It's kind of like a space-travel version of Alice in Wonderland and borrows elements from other popular sci-fi stories. For example, there’s a depressed robot named Marvin that is a parody of C3PO from Star Wars. Even "The Guide" itself is a gag on the "Encyclopedia Galactica" from the Foundation series by Isaac Asimov.

Despite it being a fun story, I think it’s a bit overhyped. It’s got a cult following and often recommended as a must-read, but I don’t think it lives up to the hype. It’s the type of book you pick up just for fun, probably as a teenager. The characters are likable, there’s a few plot twists, and it’s got some clever jokes. But it doesn’t have a lot of depth or a strong plot.
